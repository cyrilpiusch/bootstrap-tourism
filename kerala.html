Munnar

Munnar, located in the Western Ghats, is a hill station famous for its tea plantations, lush green landscapes, and pleasant weather. The mist-covered mountains, valleys, and waterfalls make it a perfect destination for nature lovers.

Fort Kochi

Fort Kochi, often referred to as the "Queen of the Arabian Sea," is a historic coastal town in the city of Kochi, Kerala. It is a melting pot of diverse cultures, with a rich colonial history that includes influences from the Portuguese, Dutch, and British. The area is known for its charming blend of old-world European architecture, narrow streets, and scenic waterfronts. Fort Kochi's vibrant arts scene, historic sites, and relaxed atmosphere make it a popular destination for tourists.

Alleppey

Alleppey, also known as Alappuzha, is famous for its enchanting backwaters and houseboat cruises. The Kerala backwaters, a network of lagoons, lakes, and rivers, provide a unique opportunity to explore the lush greenery and rural Kerala life on a houseboat. Alleppey is often referred to as the "Venice of the East."
