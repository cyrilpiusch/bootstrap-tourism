Baga Beach

Baga Beach is one of the most famous beaches in Goa, known for its vibrant nightlife, water sports, and scenic beauty. It is lined with beach shacks offering food, drinks, and live music. The beach is perfect for relaxing, swimming, and partying, making it a must-visit for tourists.

Old Goa

Old Goa is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a cultural hub of the state. The area is home to magnificent churches like the Basilica of Bom Jesus, which houses the remains of St. Francis Xavier, and the Se Cathedral, one of the largest churches in Asia. The architecture here reflects Goa's colonial past and Christian heritage.

Dudhsagar Waterfalls

Located in the Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary, Dudhsagar Waterfalls is one of India’s tallest waterfalls. The name “Dudhsagar,” meaning "sea of milk," comes from the foamy white water that cascades down from a height of about 310 meters. It's a great spot for nature lovers, trekkers, and photographers.
